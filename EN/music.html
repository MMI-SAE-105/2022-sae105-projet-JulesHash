"There's no turning back"

“There is no way to go back”, here’s how this song begins to be a worldwide success released in March 1985. This song is particularly important to me, it is one of the first music that I have known and enjoyed listening to.  Holder of numerous awards such as the Brit Award in 1986 and the Silver Disc in the UK, place of writing the song, but also number 1 sales in the United States and Canada as well as tenuse major places in the charts of other countries.

One of the end sequences of the clip of "Everybody Wants to Rule the World

A link with the World

Written at the height of the Cold War, between the United States and Russia, the song’s title would have been “Everybody wants to go to war”, however the band did not feel this title and preferred something from me didactic. Many of the words refer to a tense situation, but we can still to observe a totally different meaning. It is indeed a criticism of lust for power and control and thus of the consequences The result is destructive. This music is generally compared to the classic book “1984”. of Georges Orwell.

A picture that means everything ?

An alternative interpretation

From my point of view, I have always interpreted this music differently from its original meaning. The thirst for power and government represents me, of course, but the will to crush the world distances me from it. It should be said that this music makes me think back to childhood and the time I did not live (the eighties!). With a nostalgic melody and a rather fast tempo, the music takes us into a bygone and unknown era. We feel like we’re reliving the good times of our existence. Stop for a few moments in your ambition and remember the good memories of your life. Take the time to listen to this music and focus on what motivates you now, and how you can achieve your goals.
